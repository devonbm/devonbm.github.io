    My primary language of love is physical touch

    I connect deeply with people through touch. Hugs, hand holding, just leaning up against each other all are important. Obviously there's an element of consent to this, and I recognize not everyone wants this in return. But those who do I can connect quickly and deeply with. My secondary love language is acts of service. I show someone I care by helping out with a problem, fixing a computer, giving advice, helping navigate a difficult situation etc. Quality time ranks highly for me as well. However, words of affirmation and gifts mean virtually nothing to me (when I receive them). I have a difficult time remembering special occasions or giving thoughtful gifts, and have had to learn to do this for the people whom it really matters to. But if I don't get you a gift or say the encouraging thing, it doesn't all mean I don't care. If I want to hug you and carve out time to help you in your life, you're among my top people.
